Natural question when you visit this page is who are you?



That's what not this page is about.

What happens when you ask someone, "What is the best way to learn deep learning?" You will most likely be bombared with tons of courses online. But what the question meant was, "Where to start?" 

IMO the best way to learn data science is not through a degree, I mean look at the greatest pro you can get that is to learn from so many different courses, not bound to specific college per se. It's like go to MIT for one course, Stanford or Harvard for another, CMU or Cornell or USF next day. I mean how cool is that! And the best thing is free of cost, no travelling, no accommodation, now what can you ask more for? (Let Geff Hinton teach every course :P)

Okay enough talk, I tried to declutter almost the sources and arrange it in such that anyone can start learning about DL. But before that remember what [Richard Feynman](https://dudeperf3ct.github.io/sensei/richard-feynman/) say about knowing the name of something and understanding it.

> <span class='purple'>See that bird? It’s a brown-throated thrush, but in Germany it’s called a halzenfugel, and in Chinese they call it a chung ling and even if you know all those names for it, you still know nothing about the bird. You only know something about people; what they call the bird. Now that thrush sings, and teaches its young to fly, and flies so many miles away during the summer across the country, and nobody knows how it finds its way.</span>

Knowing the name of something doesn’t mean you understand it. The first and foremost is to understand the [difference between](https://blogs.nvidia.com/blog/2016/07/29/whats-difference-artificial-intelligence-machine-learning-deep-learning-ai/) [DL, ML](https://skymind.ai/wiki/ai-vs-machine-learning-vs-deep-learning) and [AI](https://medium.com/@mijordan3/artificial-intelligence-the-revolution-hasnt-happened-yet-5e1d5812e1e7).
